About Everwood

Who we are, how we got here, and why we work the way we do.

The owner

Dan Kowalski

Dan grew up on his father's job sites in southeast Portland, handing up shingles at ten and framing walls by sixteen. His dad ran a two-man carpentry outfit for thirty years and had one rule: if you wouldn't sign your name on it, tear it out and do it again.

After a decade framing and finishing for other contractors, Dan started Everwood in 2004 with a used truck, his father's tools, and that same rule. The first year was three decks and a kitchen. Twenty-two years later the count stands at more than 480 completed projects across Portland's east and west sides.

Dan still walks every site visit himself and still checks every job weekly. He carries Oregon CCB license #198442, and the company has been bonded and insured since the day it opened.

How we operate

What you can hold us to

Fixed written quotes

Every job gets a line-item quote within 3 business days of the site visit. If something changes mid-job, you get a written change order with a price before the work happens — never after.

One crew, start to finish

The lead carpenter who starts your job finishes it. We don't rotate crews between sites, and we don't disappear for two weeks to start someone else's kitchen.

A 5-year warranty in writing

Five years on workmanship, signed at the final walkthrough. If a door we hung starts sticking in year three, call us. We come back. That's the whole policy.
