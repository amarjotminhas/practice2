Before you ask

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a permit, and who handles it?

If the work touches structure, wiring, plumbing, or adds square footage — yes. We pull every permit ourselves and the cost is a line item in your quote, typically $500–2,500 depending on scope. Plan-reviewed work (additions, structural changes) takes 6–10 weeks for the City of Portland to approve, and we tell you that before you sign, not after. We never start regulated work without the permit in hand.

How does the payment schedule work?

A 10% deposit reserves your start date and covers initial materials. After that, payments tie to completed milestones — rough-in, drywall, finish — usually 3 to 5 payments on a mid-size job. The final 10% isn't due until you've done the walkthrough and every punch-list item is closed. We never ask for large cash sums up front; in Oregon, that's a warning sign, not a norm.

Will the timeline in the quote actually hold?

Mostly, and here's the honest version: about 8 of 10 of our jobs finish within a week of the quoted schedule. The slips come from three places — what we find when walls open (biggest one in pre-1960 houses), permit review rounds, and long-lead materials like custom cabinets or windows. We build known risks into the schedule up front and flag anything new in your Friday update the week we learn about it, with the schedule impact in writing.

What happens if I want to change something mid-project?

You get a written change order first: what changes, what it costs, what it does to the schedule. You sign before we do the work — no verbal "we'll sort it out later," because later is where disputes live. Small changes (moving an outlet, swapping a fixture) often run $150–600. Moving a wall after framing can run into thousands, and we'll tell you that plainly before you decide.

What does the 5-year warranty actually cover?

Our workmanship — anything we built or installed that fails because of how we built or installed it. A door we hung that won't close, tile we set that cracks, a deck board that cups. Call, and we come fix it at no cost. It doesn't cover product defects (those carry the manufacturer's warranty, which we register for you), normal wear, or work someone else did after us. It's in writing and signed at the final walkthrough.
