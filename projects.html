Our Work

Six recent projects with real budgets, real timelines, and the problem each one threw at us.

Kitchen remodel

Sellwood — 1926 bungalow kitchen

$58–72k · 8 weeks

Removed the wall between kitchen and dining room, new semi-custom cabinets, quartz counters, and a 9-foot island on a reinforced floor.

The challenge: live knob-and-tube wiring behind the plaster. We rewired every kitchen circuit and added a 100-amp subpanel. It cost 4 extra days, and the homeowners had the inspection paperwork before drywall went back up.

Bathroom renovation

Alberta Arts — hall bath rebuild

$32–41k · 5 weeks

Tub-to-walk-in-shower conversion with a curbless entry, heated tile floor, and a fan that actually vents outside.

The challenge: dry rot in the subfloor around the old tub — 40 square feet of it. We sistered two joists, replaced the subfloor, and used the contingency we'd already priced into the quote. No change order needed.

Room addition

Irvington — primary suite addition

$115–140k · 14 weeks

A 320 sq ft primary suite off the back of a 1912 house: bedroom, bath, and a closet that fits two people's clothes.

The challenge: historic district review required siding that matched the original profile. Nobody stocks it, so we had it custom-milled from clear cedar. From the street you can't find where old ends and new begins.

Full-home renovation

St. Johns — 1910 four-square, top to bottom

$210–260k · 22 weeks

Full gut: new electrical, plumbing, HVAC, insulation, kitchen, two baths, and refinished fir floors across three levels.

The challenge: the ridge beam had sagged an inch and a half over a century. We jacked the roof back in stages over a week and set an engineered LVL beam before any finish work started. The roofline is straight for the first time since Taft was president.

Deck & pergola

Mt. Tabor — hillside cedar deck

$24–30k · 3 weeks

A 400 sq ft cedar deck with a pergola, built on a slope that drops 8 feet across the footprint.

The challenge: the grade made poured footings a mud-season gamble. We used helical piers instead — installed in one day, engineered for the load, and no waiting on concrete cure times in a wet Portland spring.

Structural repair

Multnomah Village — settling foundation corner

$18–26k · 2 weeks

The southeast corner of a 1948 ranch had dropped almost 2 inches, cracking plaster and racking two door frames.

The challenge: the sill plate had rotted where a downspout dumped against the foundation for decades. We underpinned the corner, replaced 12 feet of sill, and rerouted the drainage so the fix stays fixed.
