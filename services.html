Services

The six kinds of work we quote, with honest budget ranges and timelines for each.

Kitchen remodels

Typically $45–90k · 6–9 weeks

The most-used room in the house, so we plan it hardest. Layout changes, wall removals with proper headers, custom or semi-custom cabinets, quartz or butcher block counters, tile backsplashes, and lighting on separate circuits.

The budget swings on cabinets and appliances. $45k gets semi-custom boxes and keeps the plumbing where it is. Past $70k you're moving walls and gas lines. We'll tell you at the site visit which side of that line your plan sits on.

Bathroom renovations

Typically $25–55k · 4–6 weeks

Tub-to-shower conversions, curbless showers, heated tile floors, proper waterproofing behind every wet wall — Schluter or hot-mop, not paint-on shortcuts. Ventilation sized to the room, vented outside, not into the attic.

Portland's older homes hide dry rot around tubs. About one bathroom in three has some. We price a contingency up front so it's a line item, not a crisis.

Room additions

Typically $90–180k · 10–16 weeks

Primary suites, family rooms, and bump-outs. Engineered foundations, framing that ties into the existing structure correctly, and rooflines that don't look bolted on. We handle design, engineering, and permits.

Plan on 6–10 weeks of permitting before ground breaks. We file early and keep you posted, but the city moves at the city's pace.

Full-home renovations

Typically $150–400k · 16–30 weeks

Down-to-the-studs work on houses from the 1900s through the 1970s. New electrical, plumbing, insulation, and mechanical while the walls are open — doing systems later costs double.

One crew, one schedule, one point of contact. You get a room-by-room scope before we start and a Friday update every week until we're done.

Decks & outdoor structures

Typically $15–45k · 2–4 weeks

Cedar, ipe, and composite decks, pergolas, and covered porches. Footings sized for Portland clay, joists at 12" centers under composite so it never feels spongy, and stainless or coated fasteners throughout.

Anything over 30" off grade needs a permit and engineered guardrails. We handle both.

Structural repair

Typically $8–60k · 1–4 weeks

Settling foundations, sagging beams, dry rot, and seismic strapping. We diagnose before we quote — sometimes with an engineer, always with a crawl through the crawl space ourselves.

If the fix is smaller than you feared, we'll say so. Half our structural calls end in a repair under $15k, not a rebuild.
